How the score is built — v2 (Growth Hybrid)

Every company is rated by five strategies, then combined into a weighted score from 0 to 100. Compared to the original value-tilted stack, this version puts Growth at the center and removes Deep Value entirely — so the engine no longer over-selects PSU banks, cyclicals, and other “cheap-but-stagnant” names.

ModelPhilosophyWeight
GrowthEarnings & revenue compounding, ROE, reinvestment, sector tailwind45%
Value InvestingBuffett — high ROE, low debt, durable margins15%
GARPLynch — PEG ratio (PE / growth)15%
DCFTwo-stage discounted cash flow15%
Sector StrengthTiered structural-growth score (NBFC/Fintech → PSU/Oil)10%

Inside the Growth Score (0–100)

Sub-componentWhat it capturesSub-weight
Revenue CAGR3–5 year top-line growth30%
Profit CAGR3–5 year earnings growth (penalised when below revenue CAGR)30%
ROE<10% low · 15–20% good · >20% excellent15%
Reinvestment RateCapex / OCF — or 1 − payout ratio as fallback10%
Sector GrowthTiered 0–10 score (Fintech 10 → PSU 3)10%
Operating LeverageProfit growth / Revenue growth — >1.2 = strong margin expansion5%

Hard filters — what “Filtered” means

Before scoring, every company must clear a quality gate. If any one of these fails, the recommendation is forced to Filtered and the reasons are listed in the detail view. This is the safety net that keeps value traps out of the rankings.

FilterThresholdWhy
Profit CAGR≥ 10%Earnings must actually be growing
ROE≥ 12%Capital is being deployed efficiently
Revenue Growth≥ 8%Top line isn’t stagnating

A "Filtered" stock isn’t necessarily bad — it’s just not a fit for a growth-focused mandate. PSU banks, slow-growing utilities, and many cyclicals end up here.

GARP / PEG calibration

  • PEG < 1.0 → excellent (90–100)
  • PEG 1.0–1.5 → acceptable (60–90)
  • PEG 1.5–2.0 → stretched (30–60)
  • PEG > 2.0 → heavy penalty (drops to 0 fast)

Recommendation rules

  • Filtered — failed any hard quality filter (see above)
  • Strong Buy — final score ≥ 75 and Growth sub-score ≥ 70
  • Buy — score 60–75
  • Hold — score 45–60
  • Avoid — score < 45

Quality flags

For stocks with Growth Score ≥ 60, the engine adds a one-line badge:

  • High Growth + High Quality — ROE ≥ 18%, D/E < 1.0, and a respectable Value or GARP score. Safer compounders.
  • Growth but Risky — growth is there, but quality is weaker (high leverage, low ROE, or stretched valuation). Treat with care.
